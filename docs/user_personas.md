## Riders

### Stressed Shift-Worker Sarah

#### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Age** | 29 |
| **Occupation** | Emergency Room Nurse (night shift) |
| **Location** | Johannesburg, South Africa |
| **Household** | Single, cohabitating with elderly mother |
| **Income Level** | Middle income (R16,000/month) |

#### Goals

- Secure dependable, safe transportation for late-night and early morning shifts
- Minimize exposure to risk or harassment on her commute
- Use a cost-effective and trustworthy ride service

#### Pain Points

- Anxiety about safety when commuting during off-peak hours
- Frequent lack of transport availability outside standard hours
- Past experiences of harassment or theft in generic ride-hailing options
- Occasional price surges straining her budget

#### User Journey

1. Opens the ChaufHER app to schedule her post-shift ride home in advance
2. Receives verified female driver information with ETA updates
3. Uses the "Share My Journey" feature so her mother can track her trip in real-time
4. Arrives home safely, providing feedback after each ride

#### Behavioral Traits

- Highly safety-conscious; vigilant during travel
- Prefers established daily routines and reliable services
- Values hassle-free, efficient digital experiences
- Engages in advocacy for women's safety among peers

#### Quotes from User Research

> "After a double shift, safety is my first priority. I want to know exactly who's picking me up, not guess."

> "It's a relief to see another woman waiting for me when I walk out those hospital doors at 4 a.m."

#### Key Scenario

Sarah finishes her shift in the early hours, confirms her ChaufHER driver's arrival, and shares the ride details with her mother, feeling reassured every step of the journey.

### Corporate Road-Warrior Nomsa

#### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Age** | 41 |
| **Occupation** | Regional Sales Director |
| **Location** | Sandton, South Africa |
| **Household** | Married, no children |
| **Income Level** | Upper-middle income (R38,000/month) |

#### Goals

- Ensure reliable, on-time travel to the airport and critical business meetings
- Maintain a professional and comfortable experience every trip
- Streamline expense tracking and ride documentation

#### Pain Points

- Frustration with inconsistent professionalism from standard ride-share drivers
- Concerns about privacy and safety, especially with late arrivals or early flight times
- Hassle and confusion in sorting receipts for business claims

#### User Journey

1. Schedules a ride to OR Tambo airport through ChaufHER's priority booking feature
2. Prefers "quiet mode" options for conference calls in transit
3. Receives automated receipts for expense reimbursement
4. Books return and daily rides for business events through the app

#### Behavioral Traits

- Organized, values punctuality and efficiency
- Prefers seamless, discreet customer service
- Seeks out brands with a strong women's safety focus

#### Quotes from User Research

> "I don't want to worry about awkward questions on my way to meetings."

> "Knowing my expenses are logged automatically is one less admin headache."

#### Key Scenario

Nomsa schedules airport transfers and meeting commutes a week in advance, with all trip receipts sent automatically to her email, supporting her fast-paced professional lifestyle.

### School-Run Guardian Aisha

#### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Age** | 35 |
| **Occupation** | High School Teacher |
| **Location** | Durban, South Africa |
| **Household** | Single mother, two children aged 7 and 10 |
| **Income Level** | Middle income (R19,000/month) |

#### Goals

- Guarantee safe, punctual school runs for her children when she is unavailable
- Gradually introduce "child only" rides with trusted female drivers
- Monitor her children's journeys in real time for added peace of mind

#### Pain Points

- Challenges managing personal commute times and school pickups simultaneously
- Anxiety about young children traveling alone with unknown drivers
- Limited transport options that cater to child safety and consent

#### User Journey

1. Registers her children as dependents, setting permissions for who can pick them up
2. Schedules regular rides for school, aftercare, and activities, with the ability to approve each journey
3. Tracks each journey's progress and receives notifications for pick-ups and drop-offs

#### Behavioral Traits

- Cautious yet adaptive, balancing independence and oversight for her children
- Strong communicator, seeks trustworthy solutions
- Frequently seeks peer validation and advice from fellow parents

#### Quotes from User Research

> "Switching to 'child only' rides was a big step, but seeing the driver profiles and live tracking reassured me."

> "My kids feel special and safe, and I have one less daily worry."

#### Key Scenario

When delayed at work, Aisha uses ChaufHER to trigger a pre-authorized pickup for her daughter, monitoring every step through the app and receiving immediate confirmation on safe arrival.

## Drivers

### Independent Earner Thandi

#### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Age** | 36 |
| **Occupation** | Full-time ChaufHER Driver (previously administrative assistant) |
| **Location** | Pretoria, South Africa |
| **Household** | Divorced, single parent to a teenage son |
| **Income Level** | R15,000–R23,000/month from driving |

#### Goals

- Maximize earning potential through regular, safe, and respectful work
- Achieve independence and financial stability on her schedule
- Be part of a mutually supportive women-only driver network

#### Pain Points

- Prior poor experiences with harassment and pay inequity in mixed-gender rideshare platforms
- Stress balancing unpredictable gig work with parental responsibilities
- Need for clear, reliable income tracking

#### User Journey

1. Completes ChaufHER's onboarding and training, accessing resources on safety and professional service
2. Logs in daily, managing her ride schedule and location for optimal trips
3. Connects with other drivers through in-app community boards for support and advice
4. Accesses clear earnings data and digital pay-out summaries

#### Behavioral Traits

- Resilient, self-motivated, and service-oriented
- Seeks respectful, professional work environments
- Engages in peer support and skill-building communities

#### Quotes from User Research

> "This platform is a game changer—I can work hard and still feel safe."

> "Driving for women, with women, makes every day more meaningful."

#### Key Scenario

Thandi juggles school drop-offs and her own work schedule, planning peak driving hours in the city, while chatting with fellow ChaufHER drivers for community and business tips.

### Flex-Hours Driver Zanele

#### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Occupation** | Boutique Assistant (primary); ChaufHER driver (side income) |
| **Location** | Cape Town, South Africa |
| **Household** | Lives with partner, no children |
| **Income Level** | R7,000/month from main job; R3,000–R6,000/month from driving |

#### Goals

- Supplement income with flexible, safe driving opportunities
- Fit driving hours around her primary work and personal life
- Experience shared empowerment and safety in a women-driver ecosystem

#### Pain Points

- Lack of transparency and unpredictability on other ride-share platforms
- Frustration with male passengers' inappropriate behavior during night shifts
- Limited access to side-hustles that respect her schedule and boundaries

#### User Journey

1. Selects available hours for ChaufHER driving, focusing mainly on evenings and weekends
2. Accepts ride requests that suit her route and timeframes
3. Reviews feedback from riders and other drivers to improve her service
4. Withdraws earnings seamlessly through the app

#### Behavioral Traits

- Flexible, entrepreneurial spirit; quick to adapt to changes
- Highly conscious of time, seeks maximized productivity per hour worked
- Prefers clear communication, regular updates, and mutual respect

#### Quotes from User Research

> "I love being able to pick up rides on my way home; it's money that fits my life."

> "No more worrying about creepy interactions—I only drive for women."

#### Key Scenario

Zanele fills her Saturday afternoons with a few short rides, earning extra money for weekend outings, while setting strict "on" and "off" hours according to her work shifts.

## Administrators

### Operational Guardian Lerato

#### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Age** | 45 |
| **Occupation** | School Secretary & Dispatcher (manages school's ChaufHER account) |
| **Location** | Bloemfontein, South Africa |
| **Household** | Married, three children |
| **Income Level** | R18,000/month |

#### Goals

- Ensure all assigned students and staff are transported safely and on time
- Easily coordinate and monitor multiple rides daily using ChaufHER admin tools
- Access detailed oversight of bookings, driver details, and journey histories

#### Pain Points

- Juggling complex daily logistics for school transportation with little admin support
- Worries about accountability, compliance, and safety for large groups of minors
- Challenges retrieving real-time updates in earlier manual or spreadsheet-based systems

#### User Journey

1. Sets up the school's ChaufHER admin dashboard with approved rider and guardian profiles
2. Schedules, authorizes, or cancels group and individual rides for staff and children
3. Monitors real-time trip statuses, driver assignments, and arrival confirmations
4. Uses exportable reports to resolve disputes and communicate with parents efficiently

#### Behavioral Traits

- Extremely organized, strong attention to detail
- Relies on clear, reliable data for decision making
- Communicative and proactive, liaises regularly with teachers, staff, and parents

#### Quotes from User Research

> "With ChaufHER's admin features, I have visibility on every student's ride—no more late pickups."

> "It takes so much pressure off knowing there's a record of every journey, driver, and handover."

#### Key Scenario

On a busy exam week, Lerato coordinates staggered pickup times, immediately reassigns rides if a driver is stuck in traffic, and updates parents instantly through the admin dashboard, ensuring seamless operations every day.

## How Personas Inform Design

These personas directly influence ChaufHER's design decisions:

| Design Guardrail | Persona Connection |
|------------------|-------------------|
| **Safety First** | Sarah needs visible safety signals; Aisha needs child transport confidence |
| **Status Clarity** | All personas need to know exactly what's happening with their ride |
| **Human Fallback** | Sarah (late-night emergencies), Aisha (child pickup issues) |
| **Repetition-Ready** | Aisha (school runs), Nomsa (business travel), Thandi (optimized routes) |
| **Predictability** | All personas benefit from simple, consistent rules |
